# BMM / Worner (Smith) — Worked Analysis Example

This is a real client-matter analysis from Lyndon's practice. Names are de-identified (Worner → Smith, Katrina → Karina, Tim → Jim).

## Context

- **Client:** Karina and Jim Smith (Australian couple)
- **Provider:** Bricks & Mortar Management Ltd (BMM) — Hong Kong-based property investment manager
- **Project:** Greek property investment via Diluca Ltd (Cyprus/Greek holding structure)
- **Agreement:** Project Management Agreement (PMA) drafted by Minter Ellison, governed by HK law
- **Duration:** 2018–2026

## Key Issues Identified

1. **Account freezes (x2)** — Cyprus parent company struck off for non-filing, NBG froze Diluca's account both times. Second freeze lasted 5+ months after final property sale, with €500K in proceeds inaccessible.

2. **Unauthorised hourly billing** — BMM charged based on staff hours outside the PMA fee structure (which only provides for Management Fee and Performance Fee).

3. **Undisclosed personal payments** — Principal (Eric) paid Diluca expenses from his own pocket without client knowledge, then sought to characterise these as interest-bearing loans at 9%.

4. **Communication failures** — Clients not told about compliance problems, not provided management accounts (Q4 2025, Q1 2026), interest rate amendment only raised while funds were frozen.

## Key Learnings for Future Analyses

- **Read the agreement's definition of "Project" first.** The scope clause determines everything — BMM's PMA defined Project to include acquisition, financing, development, leasing AND ultimate sale, which gave them lifecycle responsibility.
- **Search for admissions in correspondence.** BMM's own email admitted "we were completely in the dark" — this is gold for establishing their monitoring failure.
- **Check for recurrence.** A problem that happens twice after promises to fix it is the single strongest fact pattern for gross negligence.
- **Look for conflicted positions.** The principal paying Diluca's expenses then seeking to characterise those payments as interest-bearing loans to himself creates an obvious conflict.
- **Flag anything the client shouldn't sign.** The 9% interest amendment benefiting Eric while funds were frozen was a critical "do not sign" warning.

## Output Format Preference

Lyndon wants:
- **Markdown**, saved alongside the project brief in the project folder
- **Table-heavy** for obligations, defences, options
- **Plain English** — no legalese
- **Claim strength labels** on each breach (Strong / Clear / Arguable)
- **Actionable next steps** at the end, in priority order
- **No legal advice disclaimer** — frame as analytical summary for solicitor review
